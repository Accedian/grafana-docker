What do these alerts mean?

The analytics platform processes data through a multi-stage pipeline: collectors gather metrics and forward them through a message bus, where streaming applications consume, transform, and store the data. These alerts fire when any stage of this pipeline falls behind, stops, or shows signs of degradation.

  • Consumer lag alerts indicate that data consumers are not keeping pace with incoming data. Lag that grows continuously means the consumer is falling further behind over time.
  • Ingestion rate alerts fire when the volume of data entering the pipeline drops below expected levels, potentially indicating a collector outage or upstream data source issue.
  • Storage insertion alerts mean downstream data stores have stopped receiving new records, even though upstream stages may still be running.
  • Collector alerts fire when data collectors disconnect or accumulate a backlog of cached files waiting to be delivered.

Impact

When any stage of the ingestion pipeline is disrupted, data freshness is affected. Dashboards and reports may display stale information, and alerting rules that depend on recently ingested metrics may not fire correctly. The longer the disruption persists, the larger the data backlog that must be processed once the issue is resolved.

Investigation steps

  1. 1 Open the firing alert in Grafana and review the labels to identify the affected topic, consumer group, or collector instance.
  2. 2 Check the streaming application logs for errors. Common issues include out-of-memory conditions, serialization failures, or connectivity problems with the message bus or downstream stores.
  3. 3 Verify the message bus is healthy. Confirm that brokers are online, partitions are balanced, and no under-replicated partitions exist.
  4. 4 For collector-related alerts, verify the network path between the collector and the analytics platform. Check that the collector service is running and that cached files are not accumulating due to connectivity loss.
  5. 5 For storage insertion alerts, confirm the downstream data store is online and accepting writes. Check disk space and resource utilization on the storage nodes.

Remediation

Consumer lag / falling behind

If the streaming application is unhealthy, restart it. Once restarted, monitor the lag metric to confirm it begins decreasing. If lag continues to grow after a restart, the application may need additional resources (memory, CPU) or the data volume may have exceeded the current capacity.

Ingestion rate drop

A drop in ingestion rate typically points to an upstream issue: collectors may be offline, network connectivity may be interrupted, or the data source itself may have stopped producing. Verify collector health and network reachability before investigating the streaming application.

Storage insertion stopped

Verify the target data store is online and has sufficient disk space. Check for authentication or connectivity errors in the storage writer logs. A restart of the storage writer may be needed once the underlying issue is resolved.

Collector disconnected or files cached

A disconnected collector means data collection has stopped for that site. Verify the collector service is running and has network access to the platform. If cached files are accumulating, the collector is buffering data locally — once connectivity is restored, these files will be delivered automatically.

Verification

  • Confirm consumer lag is stable or decreasing after remediation.
  • Verify ingestion rate returns to expected levels.
  • Check that downstream data stores show recent inserts.
  • Wait for the next Grafana evaluation cycle and confirm affected alerts return to Normal.
